Year in Review December 2025

Everything I Did
in 2025

Okay so. 2025. Where do I even start? I set goals at the end of 2024 — some I hit, some I missed, and some I completely exceeded in ways I didn't expect. This is that story.

"I said I wanted to be better than I was yesterday. I think I kept that promise."

The Student Cluster Competition

This was the big one. The Student Cluster Competition (SCC) is a national competition where university teams build and run an HPC cluster under a power cap and optimise benchmarks and scientific applications. You're basically configuring servers, running Linux, tuning MPI jobs, and arguing about NixOS at 2am.

Our team was called Ctrl + Alt + Defeat — UWC. Yes, the name was intentional and yes, we were kind of defeated but also kind of not, because we placed 3rd at nationals. For a team running in the SCC for the first time the way we did it, that felt enormous.

The competition introduced me to things I had literally never heard of before: cluster networking, HPCC benchmarks, LINPACK, NixOS, and the very specific joy of watching your benchmark scores go up after three hours of tuning. I loved it. I was completely out of my depth and I loved every second of it.

The ISC Selection

After the national round, I found out I was one of 8 students selected to represent South Africa at the International Supercomputing Conference (ISC) in Germany. I genuinely had to read that email twice. This is one of the biggest HPC conferences in the world and I was going to be there representing my country. Me. The person who barely knew what a cluster was a year earlier.

Work & Leadership

Student Assistant at Centre for Student Support

From February to November I worked as a Student Assistant at the Centre for Student Support Services. I did admin, report writing, document prep — but also helped coordinate some big events including the Career Expo and Heritage Day. It sounds corporate on paper but it was genuinely interesting to see how the institutional side of a university operates.

TechForward Recruitment Officer

Through Webmax Coding Academy's TechForward Programme, I worked as a Recruitment Officer — which meant going out to under-resourced community schools and introducing learners to programming and computing. We used interactive tools and games to make it accessible. Watching a kid write their first line of code is something that doesn't get old.

Orientation Leader

At the start of 2026 (technically falls here in reflection) I stepped up from being an Orientation Guide the year before to Orientation Leader — supervising and evaluating the guides, chairing coordination meetings, and being the bridge between management and the teams on the ground. It was stressful in the best way. I learned a lot about how to manage people without micromanaging.

Academics

Second year CS hit differently from first year — but I held it together. Dean's List again, 88% GPA. The module I'm most proud of is CSC211 (Data Structures & Algorithms), where I finished 1st in the class with 97%. That led directly to being offered the tutor role in 2026, which feels like a full circle moment.

Accelerated Excellence Programme

I was selected for the Accelerated Excellence Programme — a year-long leadership development initiative for top-performing students across all faculties at UWC. We worked on presentation skills, media skills, digital presence, and community engagement. We also visited the Aurora Youth Centre in Kuils River for outreach, which was one of the most grounding experiences of the year.

Personal & Hobbies

Graphic Design

I kept at it. Slowly getting better. Still haven't "accidentally" made money from it yet, but I'm working on the accidentally part.

Adrenaline

I said I wanted adrenaline in 2025. Did I do bungee jumping? No. Did I do something scary? Competing at nationals in front of judges with a server that kept overheating counts. That's my answer and I'm sticking to it.

Work-life balance

Genuinely improving. I made time for people. I said no to things more. I remembered that rest is productive. Still a work in progress, but it's progress.

2025 was the year I stopped waiting to feel ready and just started doing things anyway. Turns out most good things happen that way.
