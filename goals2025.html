Goals December 2024

Things I Wanted
to Do in 2025

I want to push myself in 2025, but I'm keeping it realistic. I'm not trying to become the next big tech genius — just better than I was yesterday. Here's what I'm aiming for.

Tech Goals

Learn the basics of Machine Learning

Machine learning sounds intimidating, but I want to start small. Simple concepts like regression and classification, tools like Scikit-learn. Nothing crazy — just enough to get a feel for it and maybe build something cool.

Explore AI Tools

Instead of diving deep into building AI models, I'll play around with pre-built tools like Hugging Face or AI APIs. It's more about understanding how they work and seeing what's possible rather than reinventing the wheel.

Get Better at Core Programming

I'm already decent at Python and Java, but there's always room to grow. Coding challenges, LeetCode or HackerRank, solving problems better and faster.

Try a New Web Tech

I've been doing HTML and CSS, but I think it's time to explore something new. Maybe Flask or React — something that makes my websites feel more dynamic.

Build a Couple of Projects

One or two small but meaningful projects. Maybe a portfolio site or a data visualisation tool — something practical I can look back on and feel proud of.

Personal Goals

Continue Graphic Design

I've made solid progress and I want to keep that going. Experiment with new tools like Figma or dive deeper into Illustrator. It's still a hobby, but one I really enjoy and want to get better at.

Seek Adrenaline-Fuelled Adventures

I am not the most daring person I know and this is going to be way out of my comfort zone — but you're only 21 once right? Maybe bungee jumping, zip-lining, or rock climbing. Something that scares me a little.

Travel and Explore

I want to get out more and see new places. Whether it's a quick nearby trip or something bigger, I just want to experience more of what's out there.

Improve Work-Life Balance

Balancing everything was tough at the beginning of my degree. I sacrificed my personal life for an 80+ GPA and while it paid off, it also taught me the importance of balance. This year I want to make time for hobbies, relaxation, and just living life a little more. Productivity is important, but staying sane while doing it is more important.
