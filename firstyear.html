This is how my 2024 went

Python

Learning Python felt refreshing after my experience with Visual Basic. I already knew the basics of programming, but Python felt like a simpler, more elegant way to write code. It wasn't just about syntax — it made problem-solving feel smoother, like taking a deep breath after a tough day. When I started exploring NumPy for data analytics, it all clicked. Python wasn't just a language; it was a tool that made me realize how much I love finding patterns and making sense of data. It opened doors to an area I now see myself diving deeper into.

Java

Java was a bit of a challenge at first. Coming from Visual Basic and Python, Java's syntax felt more rigid. But as I stuck with it, I started to understand its structure and why it's so widely used. The rules made sense as they pushed me to think more about the long-term stability of my code. It wasn't just about getting something to work — it was about making sure it worked well. I started to see the appeal for larger projects and systems. It might not have been love at first sight, but it's definitely earned my respect. When all is said and done I will pick Java over Python any day, we have become best buddies and I can see us in a long term relationship.

HTML & CSS

HTML was my introduction to the web, and it was a straightforward but essential lesson. At first, it seemed too simple — like I wasn't really coding, just building the skeleton of a website. But the more I worked with it, the more I realized how important that structure is. It was like learning the foundation of a house — it's not glamorous, but nothing works without it.

CSS is where the design magic happened. After learning HTML, CSS felt like the brush strokes that brought a website to life. At first, I found it a bit tricky, especially with things like positioning and alignment. But once I got the hang of it, it felt satisfying to see my pages not just work, but actually look good. It's where I learned the value of aesthetics in a digital world.

First Year

So I started my first year in Computer Science this year. I initially did not even want to do Computer Science if I am going to be honest — I wanted something to do with Math so my main goal was to transfer in second year. Turns out Computer Science is just another glorified Math degree so I actually started to enjoy it so much I forgot to apply for a transfer.

Rating all my modules: MAT105 gets a 10/10, I absolutely enjoyed Calculus but Linear Algebra was a bit challenging (which I actually loved). Stats was not as challenging, I was kinda bored so I give it a 6/10. COS101 (Introduction to Computer Science) — I enjoyed Java more than Python but the overall course content was interesting so I give that an 8/10. I did not find first year to be as difficult as I thought it would be, it was a breeze and I currently have a GPA of 89.5% — but that's for two modules and will probably drop cause of my one Math paper lol.

Moving To Cape Town

I moved to Cape Town for uni. Was it planned? No, but it has been an experience. I struggled to adjust to the weather at first because summer gets really hot — constant headaches, and why is the sun on full blast at like 7am? I had constant jumpscares thinking I was late for school cause of the light and heat but it would literally be 7am. Winter was not any better — I did not know it rained in winter till I was already here and I am not a winter person, let alone a wet winter? However, it really is a beautiful city and I would not mind staying here permanently.

Graphic Design

I have been meaning to get in touch with my artsy side for a while now but school and other commitments kept getting in the way (pure laziness honestly). It wasn't till I realised that hobbies are what make a person and give you personality — and if someone were to ask me to tell them about myself, I would have nothing. If I were a dish, I would probably be a plain vegetable soup, but it's time to change that! I have combined the two things I love, computers and graphics, and have started designing. I am hoping to make money out of it soon though lol — like those people who "accidentally" make money from their hobbies.

Well this is it! Hope you had fun reading this 😊
