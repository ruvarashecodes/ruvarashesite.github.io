Throwback 15 November 2024

How My
2024 Went

This was first year. I did not expect to be here. I did not even want to study Computer Science if I'm going to be honest — I wanted something heavily Math-focused and planned to transfer after first year. Then I found out CS is basically a glorified Math degree, started enjoying it, and forgot to apply for the transfer.

Python

Learning Python felt refreshing after my experience with Visual Basic. I already knew the basics of programming, but Python felt like a simpler, more elegant way to write code. It made problem-solving feel smoother, like taking a deep breath after a tough day. When I started exploring NumPy for data analytics, it all clicked. Python wasn't just a language — it was a tool that made me realise how much I love finding patterns and making sense of data.

Java

Java was a challenge at first. Coming from Visual Basic and Python, Java's syntax felt rigid. But as I stuck with it, I started to understand its structure and why it's so widely used. The rules made sense — they pushed me to think about the long-term stability of my code, not just getting something to run. It might not have been love at first sight, but it earned my respect. When all is said and done I will pick Java over Python any day. We are best buddies now and I can see us in a long-term relationship.

HTML & CSS

HTML was my introduction to the web. At first it felt too simple — like I wasn't really coding, just building the skeleton of a page. But the more I worked with it, the more I appreciated how important structure is. It's the foundation of a house — not glamorous, but nothing works without it. CSS is where the design magic happened. Once I got the hang of positioning and alignment, there was something genuinely satisfying about making a page look good, not just function.

First Year Overall

I genuinely did not find first year as difficult as I thought it would be. Rating my modules: MAT105 (Calculus) gets a 10/10 — I absolutely loved it. Linear Algebra was challenging, which I actually enjoyed. Stats felt too easy, 6/10. COS101 was interesting overall — I enjoyed Java more than Python but the full course earns an 8/10.

I finished with a 89.5% GPA across two modules, which will probably shift when the full year closes — but I'm proud of it. I was nominated for Top First-Year at the Rector's Awards and placed in the top 3 first-year undergraduates at UWC. That was a good day.

Moving to Cape Town

I moved to Cape Town for uni. Was it planned? No. But it has been an experience. Summer here is genuinely relentless — the sun is on full blast at 7am and I had constant jumpscares thinking I was late for class. Winter hit differently too: nobody told me it rains in Cape Town in winter. I was not prepared. But it's a beautiful city and I wouldn't mind being here permanently.

Graphic Design

I finally started. I've been meaning to get in touch with my artsy side for years and school kept getting in the way — or honestly, I kept getting in my own way. I realised that hobbies are what make a person and I didn't want to be a plain vegetable soup anymore. I've combined the two things I love — computers and art — and started designing. I'm hoping to make money from it accidentally, like those people on the internet. Working on it.

Well, this is it! Hope you had fun reading this 😊
