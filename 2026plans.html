Reflection · Looking Ahead March 2026

From First Year
to Here

When I started this website, I wrote: "It's just me and my basic HTML skills against the world." I meant it as a joke, but looking back, it was kind of true. I was new to Cape Town, new to CS, and putting words on a page with no CSS because I didn't know how CSS worked yet.

A lot has changed since then.

"I went from not wanting to study Computer Science, to representing South Africa at an international HPC conference. I don't think first-year me would believe that."

What I've grown into

Co-Founding the HPC Club

After the Student Cluster Competition, I co-founded the UWC High Performance Computing Club. The goal is simple: introduce students to the HPC world — Linux, networking, cluster computing, HPC hardware — and prepare teams for the annual Student Cluster Competition.

I wish this club had existed when I was starting out. I had to figure so much out as I went, and while that was a valuable experience, a lot of students don't get the chance to figure it out at all because they don't even know this field exists. That's what the club is trying to change. We're mentoring members through to the national round and I am very genuinely excited to see where this goes.

Leading the UWC Mathematics Club

I've been President of the UWC Mathematics Club since 2025. We provide free mathematics tutoring to high school students who can't afford private tutors, and we also run food drives and campus soup days during exam periods. It started as a club and turned into something I care about deeply.

There's something very specific about watching a student suddenly understand something they'd been struggling with for months. Tutoring does that for me over and over, and the Maths Club is where I get to do it at scale.

Tutoring DSA

Getting 1st in CSC211 with 97% was one thing. Being asked to tutor the module was another. I spend a lot of time now thinking about how to explain things — not just knowing them, but making them accessible. It's hard and it's one of the most useful skills I'm building.

Where I'm going in 2026

International Supercomputing Conference

Representing South Africa at the International Supercomputing Conference is happening this year. I'm nervous and excited in equal measure. I want to absorb everything — the talks, the people, the research, the scale of it all. I've never been to Europe. I'm going for HPC. That's a sentence I did not expect to write.

Third Year

Third year is going to be the most academically demanding year so far. I want to keep the GPA up and I want to go deeper into the things I actually love — algorithms, systems, data. I'm also hoping to do something meaningful with machine learning this year, even if it's just getting comfortable with the fundamentals.

Build more things

This website is technically one of my projects. I want more of those. I want to build things that work, that I can show people, that solve actual problems. I don't need them to be impressive — I just need them to be real.

Keep the balance

I've said this before and I'll keep saying it: I want to do well academically without losing myself in the process. I did that in first year. I don't want to do it again. Rest is part of the plan, not the reward at the end of it.

If first-year Rufaro could see this — the clubs, the competition, the tutoring, the international conference — I think she'd be proud. And a little shocked. And she'd ask why the site still doesn't have dark mode yet. It does now. We're growing.
